Pricing intelligence

We can see what care actually costs.

Most plans price care off a rented network table they can't see into. We maintain our own national pricing warehouse — what the major carriers negotiate and what hospitals charge in cash — so we can find the true price of a procedure and steer members to the better one.

[ The core ]

Two prices, side by side.

Every plan asks the same two questions: what do the carriers actually pay for this, and what's the cash price? We answer both — for the same procedure, in the same place.

Negotiated

What plans actually pay

Real negotiated dollar rates pulled from carrier transparency files — nine major carriers and PPO networks, including the big national carriers and the largest rental networks — across hundreds of thousands of facilities and providers.

Cash

What care costs in cash

Hospital cash and self-pay prices nationwide, straight from hospital price-transparency files — the number a member would pay without insurance.

Put them next to each other and route the member to the cheaper one. That cross-payer, cash-versus-negotiated comparison — at national scale — is the hard part, and it's what we own.

[ How we read the market ]

Real distributions, not blended averages.

01

Medians with percentile spreads

The 25th-to-75th-percentile range by procedure, by facility, and by ZIP — so you see the real spread in a local market, not one national number.

02

Anchored to real identity

Every rate tied to a named provider and tax-ID (national NPPES identity) and benchmarked against Medicare reference rates.

03

Refreshed monthly

Rebuilt from the latest carrier and hospital transparency files each month, so plan and cost decisions run on current pricing.

[ Scale ]

Billions of distinct price points, covering the large majority of medical spend.
